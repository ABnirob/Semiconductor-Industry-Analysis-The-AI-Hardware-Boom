## Qualitative Competitive Moats Review
Market concentration varies by layer, but overall industry consolidation remains high. With an aggregate HHI of 1950, the sector is classified as highly concentrated. In advanced GPUs, NVIDIA operates a de-facto duopoly with AMD. This concentration is protected by insurmountable structural entry barriers: an advanced fab costs upward of $20 billion, and state-of-the-art microarchitecture design requires thousands of specialized engineers.

## Competitive Positioning Insights
1. Threat of New Entrants is practically zero for leading-edge silicon due to astronomical capital thresholds and patent thickets.
2. Bargaining Power of Suppliers is highly concentrated in specialized equipment providers (such as ASML) and sole-source foundries.
3. Competitive Rivalry is intense among legacy giants but moderated in the premium tier by product positioning and ecosystem locks.

## Student Observations & Analysis Notes
NVIDIA's de facto monopoly in AI training hardware gives them unmatched pricing power. Regulators are starting to look at this, which could create a future legal/compliance bottleneck.
