## Qualitative Risk Landscape Review
The industry faces unprecedented concentric risks. Geopolitically, the reliance on a single island (Taiwan) for advanced fabrication is a critical single-point of failure. On the tech front, photolithography physics are reaching atomic boundaries, meaning wafer costs are rising rather than falling at smaller physical sizes. Finally, commercial viability relies heavily on the continued return-on-investment of large-language model software.

## Vulnerability Hotspots & Takeaways
1. Systemic dependency on ASML EUV machinery represents a massive operational bottleneck for all fabricators.
2. Sovereign localized supply chain nodes are extremely expensive and lag TSMC's manufacturing yield efficiency by years.
3. A slowdown in cloud AI capital expenditure remains the primary stock catalyst threat over a 24-month horizon.
