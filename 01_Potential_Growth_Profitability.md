## Qualitative Market Potential Analysis
The semiconductor industry is entering a golden secular epoch. Driven by structural shifts toward advanced deep-learning networks, high-performance datacenters, and edge computing, the market size is on track to eclipse $1 Trillion by 2030. Profitability is skewed strongly toward leading-node developers that can capture the hardware software co-design moat (such as NVIDIA's CUDA platform). However, high CapEx and specialized supply constraints restrict overall flexibility.

## Sector Observations
1. AI workloads have decoupled high-performance computing silicon from consumer hardware cycles (like PCs and mobile).
2. The software stack (e.g., compilers, libraries, runtime environments) acts as a far stronger margin protector than raw hardware silicon architecture.
3. Governments are subsidizing local fabs, creating structural redundancies that could trigger overcapacity in trailing-edge nodes in the medium-term.
